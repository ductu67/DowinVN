⚙️ Products & Specifications

The three models differ in mold size, output and scale of application:

• DW-1000 — 1000×800mm mold, 120 molds/hour, 22 kW. Suited to small and medium foundries.
• DW-1200 — 1200×1000mm mold, 180 molds/hour, 30 kW. The most popular model, well-balanced between cost and performance.
• DW-1600 — 1600×1200mm mold, 250 molds/hour, 45 kW. For large factories and highly automated production lines.

DW green sand molding machines are suitable for casting grey iron, ductile iron, low-carbon steel and aluminium alloys — the most common metals in machinery parts, automotive, agricultural and industrial casting in Vietnam. Metals requiring exceptionally high pouring temperatures (such as titanium) are not compatible with standard green sand molds.
With proper scheduled maintenance, DW machines have an average service life of 15–20 years. Machine frames are made from heavy structural steel; hydraulic/pneumatic cylinders are sourced from leading suppliers. Wear items (bearings, seals, solenoid valves) are replaced periodically and are always held in DOWIN's warehouse.

🔧 Installation & Operation

Minimum floor space for each model (including operating and maintenance clearance):

• DW-1000: approx. 25–30 m² (5×5m)
• DW-1200: approx. 35–40 m² (6×6m)
• DW-1600: approx. 50–60 m² (8×7m)

A full production line (sand mixer + conveyor + cooling) requires at least 150–300 m² depending on configuration. DOWIN engineers will survey and plan the optimal layout free of charge.
A single DW machine requires 1–2 operators. A fully automated closed-loop line can be operated with 3–4 people (compared with 10–15 using manual methods). This is one of the biggest advantages of automation — a 70% reduction in labour while increasing output.
DOWIN will supply machine foundation drawings before delivery. Typical requirements:

• Concrete slab minimum 200–300mm thick (depending on model)
• Concrete grade ≥ B25 (M300)
• Anchor bolt pockets to drawing
• Cable conduit and compressed air pipework to machine position

If the existing workshop floor meets these conditions, no additional foundation is needed.
