Servo vs. Hydraulic Molding Machines — Real Cost Comparison

📅 05/12/2024 ⏱️ 7 min read ✍️ DOWIN Engineering

When comparing these two types, most people look only at the list price. A servo machine costs roughly 20–30% more than a hydraulic machine of the same size. But if you look at the 5-year total cost of ownership, the picture changes completely.

This article analyses real data from three foundries in northern Vietnam currently operating both machine types — one legacy hydraulic unit and one newly installed servo.

The fundamental mechanical difference

⚡ Servo
  • Variable-frequency drive (VFD) servo motor
  • Draws power only according to actual load
  • Precise digital clamping pressure control
  • Lower heat output → hydraulic oil lasts longer
  • Quieter operation, less vibration
🔧 Fixed hydraulic
  • Motor runs continuously at full power
  • Fixed energy draw whether pressing or waiting
  • Pressure adjusted by mechanical valves
  • High heat generation → oil degrades faster
  • Higher noise and vibration levels

5-year TCO difference: ~VND 475 million in favour of servo. Despite a 25% higher purchase price, servo machines typically recover that premium within 18–24 months.

When does traditional hydraulic still make sense?

Hydraulic machines still have advantages in certain situations:

  • Foundry runs only a few hours per day (under 4–6 hours) — energy savings don't offset the price difference
  • Very tight initial capital budget with no capacity for a longer payback period
  • Foundry already has a skilled hydraulic maintenance team, keeping real maintenance costs low

Conclusion

If your foundry runs 8 hours per day or more, a servo machine is almost always the more economical choice over a 5-year horizon. Hydraulic is only rational for low-utilisation foundries or where the upfront cost is a genuine barrier.
