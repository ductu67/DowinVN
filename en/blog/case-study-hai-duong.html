Case Study: Hai Duong Foundry Upgrade — Output Up 85%

📅 18/11/2024 ⏱️ 5 min read 📍 Hai Duong 🏭 Machinery parts casting

Background: Order pressure, but the old machine couldn't keep up

An agricultural machinery parts foundry in Hai Duong (name withheld on request) had been operating for over 12 years with an old hydraulic molding line. In 2023, orders from assembly plants surged — customers demanded a 30% output increase within 6 months. The old machine was running at 80 molds/hour, suffering frequent downtime from hydraulic failures and high energy consumption.

Management faced a clear decision: upgrade the machine or buy a second one? After analysing available floor space and operating costs, the choice was made to replace the old machine with a modern servo unit.

The Challenges

  • Required output: Customers needed at least 130–150 molds/hour to fulfil new orders
  • Limited floor space: No room in the workshop for a second machine
  • Minimum downtime: The line could not stop for more than 5 days during peak season
  • Energy costs: Electricity bills represented 18% of production costs — a reduction was needed

The Solution: DW-1200 Servo

After a DOWIN engineer surveyed the site and analysed the technical requirements, the DW-1200 was selected because:

  • Output of 180 molds/hour — exceeds the 150 molds/hour requirement with a comfortable safety margin
  • Overall footprint 4,200×3,000×3,200mm — fits the old machine's space (4,500×3,200mm)
  • Variable-frequency servo motor — significantly lower energy consumption than fixed-speed hydraulic
  • Servo PLC with 10" touch screen — intuitive to operate, minimal retraining required

The installation schedule was planned to coincide with a public holiday to minimise production losses. Removing the old machine and installing the DW-1200 took 3 days. Day 4 was used for trial runs and calibration. Day 5 for handover and operator training.

Results after 6 months of operation

Six months after installation, the foundry reported:

  • Actual output: 148–155 molds/hour (98% of committed specification)
  • Energy consumption: Reduced from 0.38 kWh/mold to 0.26 kWh/mold (32% reduction)
  • Mold defect rate: Reduced from 4.2% to 1.8% due to more uniform clamping pressure
  • Unplanned stoppages: 0 in the first 6 months (vs. 3–4 per month on the old machine)
  • Operating staff: Reduced from 2 to 1 — the second operator was reassigned to another department
"We didn't expect the servo machine to save so much electricity. I always thought it was just about running faster, but a monthly energy saving of over VND 15 million was a very pleasant surprise. After 6 months, we ordered a second DW-1200 for our second line."

— Production Director, Hai Duong parts foundry

Key takeaways

This case study illustrates three important points when upgrading a foundry:

  1. Energy costs are the most underestimated factor when comparing old and new machines. A servo machine may cost 20–30% more upfront but delivers a faster payback through energy savings.
  2. Scheduling installation during public holidays is the most effective way to minimise production losses.
  3. Output headroom matters — don't buy a machine that's just sufficient. The DW-1200 (180 molds/hour) replacing a 150 molds/hour requirement gives the foundry comfortable headroom and the ability to handle order spikes.
