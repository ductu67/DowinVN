Sand Molding Machine Maintenance Schedule — DOWIN Standard Guide

📅 10/01/2025 ⏱️ 6 min read ✍️ DOWIN Engineering

According to data collected from DOWIN's customer foundries, over 90% of unplanned machine stoppages result from neglecting simple routine maintenance tasks. A worn oil seal not replaced on schedule can lead to an oil leak, sand contamination, and a 2–3 day shutdown — rather than a 30-minute seal replacement.

This article provides the standard maintenance schedule applicable to the full DW-1000/1200/1600 range.

⚠️ Safety notice: Switch off mains power, release all compressed air pressure, and wait for the hydraulic system to cool completely before carrying out any maintenance work. Always lock out the isolator and attach a "Maintenance in progress" tag at the control panel.

Daily maintenance (Before each production shift — 15 minutes)

ItemHow to checkStandard
Hydraulic oil levelRead the sight glass on the tankBetween MIN and MAX marks
Compressed air inlet pressureRead the pressure gauge0.6–0.7 MPa (DW-1200)
FRL compressed air filterCheck water bowlDrain if more than half full
Guide rail lubricationVisual and tactile checkOil film still present
Unusual soundsListen during trial runNo knocking or squealing
Pattern conditionDirect visual inspectionNo chipped edges, no hardened sand adhesion

Weekly maintenance (1 hour)

ItemTask
Full machine clean-downBlow off sand deposits with compressed air. Clean cylinders, guide rails and all gaps
Foundation and frame boltsRetighten with a spanner — vibration during operation loosens bolts weekly
Wiring and limit switchesInspect insulation, ensure cables are not pinched or abrading against surfaces
Joints and hinges lubricationApply high-temperature grease to all lubrication points per the machine drawing
Pneumatic cylinder sealsCheck for air leaks using a soap solution or by listening for hissing

Monthly maintenance (3–4 hours)

ItemTask
Replace hydraulic oil filter elementReplace with the correct element code. Do not reuse old elements even after washing
Check hydraulic oil qualitySample and check colour — milky (emulsified) or very black → replace entire oil charge
Calibrate pressure and travel sensorsCompare PLC readings against mechanical gauges — if deviation >5%, adjust
Check solenoid valvesTest coil resistance and confirm correct open/close operation
Clean oil heat exchangerDrain sediment from tank base. Clean heat-sink fins with compressed air

Annual maintenance (1–2 days — recommend using DOWIN technicians)

  • Replace all hydraulic oil and clean the oil tank
  • Check and align parallelism of press platen and squeeze head
  • Replace main cylinder seals if minor leakage is detected
  • Inspect electric motors (winding resistance, insulation resistance)
  • Update PLC firmware to the latest version
  • Back up all PLC programs and configuration parameters
  • Inspect and retighten all terminal connections inside the control cabinet

Signs requiring immediate action (don't wait for the next scheduled visit)

  • 🔴 Hydraulic oil leak visible on the workshop floor
  • 🔴 Clamping pressure not reaching set value (deviation >10%)
  • 🔴 Unusual metal-on-metal impact noise during pressing
  • 🔴 HMI displaying sensor or valve error codes
  • 🔴 Mold misalignment or uneven parting line
  • 🟡 Oil temperature exceeding 60°C (normal range 40–55°C)
  • 🟡 Cycle time slower than normal by more than 15%
